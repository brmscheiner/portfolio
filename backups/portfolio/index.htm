Welcome to my little corner of the internet. My name is Ben Scheiner, and for the moment this site functions as my virtual CV.

I am an aspiring developer with professional experience in data science and visualization. I spent the beginning of 2016 at the Recurse Center and worked at the Federal Reserve from 2013 to 2015. At Trinity University, I double majored in Math and Theoretical Economics. I have worked with Python, Javascript, C, and Matlab, but I love learning new languages too!

At school I studied higher level statistics and econometrics. At the Federal Reserve, I had the opportunity to apply many of the techiques I learned to real world problems. I have extensive experience with regression analysis and exposure to Bayesian statistics, impulse response functions, and stochastic processes.

I am passionate about bringing data to life. At the Federal Reserve, I created chart exhibits that were used in papers and briefings to the Board of Governors. At the Recurse Center, I became familiar with D3 and P5 so that I could create interactive visualizations for the web. My most recent project is Ideogram.

Python is my strongest language. At the Federal Reserve, I used it to write data processing scripts. Ideogram analyzes Python code and is written in Python. I am familiar with generators, decorators, and many libraries, including requests, ast, pandas, and matplotlib.

I created my first website when I was 16, but only started writing my own Javascript recently. I have become familiar with D3, P5, and JQuery.

At the Recurse Center, I learned C and implemented Lempel-Ziv-Welch (LZW) compression.

At the Federal Reserve, I implemented models of monetary policy contagion in Matlab. In addition, I used Matlab for many data requests and charts, including some 3D visualizations.
